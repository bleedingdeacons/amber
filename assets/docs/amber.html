🟠 Amber Admin Guide

Step-by-step directions for every task in the WordPress admin — managing intergroup meetings, members, positions, groups, and the public directory.

What Amber adds to WordPress

Amber installs a top-level Intergroup menu in the WordPress admin sidebar and adds three dashboard widgets. Everything you need to run Bristol & District Intergroup is in that menu — positions, members, group meetings, and intergroup business meeting records.

Changes made through the admin update the public-facing website automatically. There is no separate publishing step when updating an officer or fixing a meeting time.

⚠️
GDPR reminder. The Scrutiny plugin logs every access to personal member data. Use only the information you need, for the purpose you need it for. Do not share personal details held in the system outside of it.

Logging In

  1. Go to your site's WordPress login page — typically https://aa-bristol.org/wp-admin.
  2. Enter your admin username and password.
  3. Click Log In. You will land on the WordPress Dashboard.
  4. Look for the Intergroup menu item in the left-hand sidebar. If you cannot see it, your account may not have the required permissions — contact the site administrator.
💡
The admin URL and your login credentials are separate from the public-facing website. Your credentials should be kept private and not shared.

Dashboard Widgets

The WordPress Dashboard (the home screen after logging in) shows three at-a-glance widgets. These give you a live summary without opening any sub-page.

Positions & Members

Lists every position alphabetically with the current holder's anonymous name and a colour-coded rotation badge. Vacant and overdue positions are immediately visible. Clicking a position title opens its edit screen.

Groups & Meetings

Lists all meetings sorted by day of the week, then by start time. Each meeting card shows the group name, location, contacts, and a reconciliation badge indicating whether the meeting appears in the national AAGBDB listing. Days can be collapsed by clicking the header.

Intergroup Meetings

Lists past and upcoming intergroup business meetings, sorted most recent first. Each card shows the date, total attendance count, and the groups and officers who attended. Click a date to open the full meeting record.

Creating an Intergroup Meeting Record

Intergroup › Intergroup Meetings › Add New

Create the meeting record at least a week before the meeting date. This gives time to check which member is currently each group's GSR before attendance is recorded on the day.

  1. Go to Intergroup › Intergroup Meetings in the sidebar.
  2. Click Add New at the top of the page.
  3. In the Title field, enter the meeting name — use the format: Intergroup Meeting — March 2026.
  4. Fill in the Date field using the date picker.
  5. Leave the Group Attendees and Officers Attending fields empty for now.
  6. Click Publish.
FieldWhat to enter
Title* The meeting name. Recommended: Intergroup Meeting — Month Year.
Date* The date the meeting takes place. Use the date picker to select the correct date.
Group attendees The AA groups who sent a representative. When you select a group, the GSR's anonymous name appears in brackets automatically — you do not type it.
Officers attending The officers present. Each officer's current position title appears in brackets in the selector.

Recording Attendance on the Day

Intergroup › Intergroup Meetings › Edit

When the meeting begins, open the record you created in advance and update it as people arrive.

  1. Go to Intergroup › Intergroup Meetings.
  2. Find today's meeting in the list and click its title to open the edit screen.
  3. In the Group Attendees field, click inside the selector and choose each group as its representative arrives. The GSR's anonymous name appears in brackets — this is pulled automatically from the member records.
  4. In the Officers Attending field, select each officer who is present. Their position title appears alongside their name.
  5. Click Update. Structured attendance records are created in the background automatically — one per group, one per officer.
✓
Proxy attendance. If a group sent a proxy instead of the GSR, record the group as attending and note the proxy name in the attendance detail record. See Viewing Attendance.
⚠️
Officer elected during the meeting? If someone is voted into a new position during the meeting, update their member record immediately after the vote. The system will update today's attendance record automatically. Historical records from past meetings are not changed.

Viewing Attendance Detail

Intergroup › Attendance

The Attendance page shows full attendance records for any past intergroup meeting. Use this as source data when writing the attendance section of the minutes.

  1. Go to Intergroup › Attendance in the sidebar.
  2. Use the dropdown at the top of the page to select the meeting you want to view.
  3. Two tables appear below the selector:
    • Group attendance — group name, GSR anonymous name, whether a proxy attended, and the proxy's name.
    • Officer attendance — position title and officer's anonymous name.
💡
The attendance data is stored at the point of recording, so if an officer's name or position changes later, the historical record is preserved as it was on the day.

Editing a Member

Intergroup › Members › [Member name]
  1. Go to Intergroup › Members.
  2. Find the member using the search box at the top right, or sort the list by clicking Anonymous Name, Service Position, Is GSR?, or Homegroup in the column headers.
  3. Click the member's name to open their record.
  4. Make your changes.
  5. Click Update.
⚠️
Changing an anonymous name. Type the new name in the Anonymous Name field — the system checks it is unique before saving. The post title updates automatically. Positions and attendance records linked to this member will reflect the new name.

Updating a Member's Contact Details

Intergroup › Members › [Member name]
  1. Open the member record (see Editing a Member).
  2. Scroll down to the Personal Email and Mobile Number fields.
  3. Update as needed.
  4. Click Update.
💡
Personal contact details are never shown publicly. They are logged by Scrutiny every time they are accessed, in line with GDPR requirements. Updating these fields requires the scrutiny_edit_personal_data capability — without it the fields are read-only.

Setting or Removing GSR Status

Intergroup › Members › [Member name]

GSR status is separate from holding an intergroup officer position. A member can be a GSR without any officer role, and can hold an officer role without being a GSR.

  1. Open the member record.
  2. Find the Is GSR checkbox and tick or untick it.
  3. Click Update.

After saving, the GSR's name will appear in brackets next to their home group when you next open an intergroup meeting record. If there is an intergroup meeting today, the attendance record is updated immediately.

Creating a Position

Intergroup › Positions › Add New

Position records define each service role — Chair, Secretary, Treasurer, and all liaison positions. Setting these up correctly powers both the rotation dashboard and the public directory.

  1. Go to Intergroup › Positions.
  2. Click Add New.
  3. In Long Name, type the full formal name — e.g. Intergroup Secretary. The system checks for uniqueness as you type.
  4. In Short Description, enter a brief label — e.g. Secretary. This appears in admin tables and shortcodes.
  5. Set Minimum Sobriety in months. Core officer roles typically require 24 months.
  6. Set Term Years — usually 2.
  7. Enter the Position Email — the generic role-based address shown publicly, e.g. secretary@aa-bristol.org.
  8. Write a Summary of the role's responsibilities. This appears on the public position page.
  9. Click Publish.
FieldWhat to enter
Long name*Full formal name. Must be unique. Drives the post title automatically — do not edit the title directly.
Short description*A brief label for admin tables and the position shortcodes.
SummaryRich-text description of duties. Displayed on the public page via the [position_summary] shortcode.
Position email*The generic role-based email address. This is public — not the officer's personal email.
Minimum sobriety (months)*Minimum continuous sobriety required to hold this position.
Term length (years)*Standard term length. Used to calculate rotation dates.
Current memberThe member currently holding the role. Leave blank when first creating the position.
Rotation dateWhen the current officer's term ends. Set when they are assigned.

Assigning a Member to a Position

After a member is elected, link them to their position. You can do this from either the position record or the member record — both work.

From the position record

Intergroup › Positions › [Position name]
  1. Go to Intergroup › Positions and open the position record.
  2. In the Current Member field, click the selector and choose the new officer. Their anonymous name appears in the picker.
  3. Set the Rotation Date to the expected end of their term — typically their election date plus the term length in years.
  4. Click Update.

From the member record

Intergroup › Members › [Member name]
  1. Go to Intergroup › Members and open the member record.
  2. In Intergroup Position, select the position they now hold.
  3. Set the Rotation Date.
  4. Click Update.
💡
Either approach works — the system links positions and members bidirectionally. If you edit both sides in the same session, save one before opening the other.
⚠️
Replacing one officer with another. Always clear the outgoing officer first, then assign the new one. This prevents a position appearing to have two holders simultaneously.

Marking a Position as Vacant

Intergroup › Positions › [Position name]

When an officer leaves and no replacement has been elected, clear the position so it shows correctly on the dashboard and the public directory.

  1. Go to Intergroup › Positions and open the position record.
  2. In the Current Member field, click the × next to the member's name to remove the link.
  3. Click Update.

The position immediately shows a Vacant badge on the dashboard and on the public directory. The email link on the public page is removed automatically.

Reading the Rotation Badges

The Positions & Members dashboard widget shows a status badge on every position. Check this at the start of each Intergroup business meeting to identify items that need to go on the agenda.

BadgeMeaningAction
Filled Officer in post, more than 3 months remaining. No action needed.
Soon (Xm) 3 months or fewer remaining in current term. Add to the agenda as an upcoming rotation. Begin succession planning.
Due Rotation date is this month. Thank the outgoing officer. Hold an election at this meeting or the next.
Overdue Rotation date has passed. Officer may be continuing in an acting capacity. Add as a priority agenda item. An election is needed immediately.
Vacant No officer is currently assigned. Raise as urgent. The public directory shows this position as vacant.
No Rotation Position is filled but no rotation date has been set. Ask the officer for their expected end date and update the record.

Adding a New Group

Groups are stored as a custom post type. Navigate to it using the WordPress sidebar — it may appear under a custom label depending on your site setup, typically alongside or below the Intergroup menu.

  1. Navigate to the Groups post type in the sidebar.
  2. Click Add New.
  3. Enter the group name as the post title.
  4. Fill in the contact fields — email address, phone number, and website.
  5. Add at least one named contact in the Contacts section — name and phone number.
  6. Set the District ID if applicable.
  7. Click Publish.

Updating Group Contact Details

  1. Navigate to the Groups post type and find the group you want to update.
  2. Click the group name to open its record.
  3. Update the relevant fields.

Adding an AA Meeting

  1. Navigate to the Meetings post type in the sidebar.
  2. Click Add New.
  3. Enter the meeting name as the post title — e.g. Clifton Vale Thursday Evening.
  4. Set the Day of Week, Start Time, and End Time.
  5. Link the meeting to its Group using the group selector field.
  6. Link it to a Location, or create a new location record if the venue doesn't exist yet.
  7. If the meeting is online, tick Online Meeting and enter the Zoom or video link.
  8. Select the Meeting Types — Open, Closed, Speaker, Step, etc.
  9. Click Publish.

Reading the Reconciliation Badges

Each meeting card in the Groups & Meetings dashboard shows a badge indicating whether the meeting appears in the national AAGBDB listing. This helps identify meetings that may need attention.

BadgeMeaningAction
AAGBDB Confident match — same day, time, and similar name in the national listing. No action needed.
AAGBDB ~ Partial match — day and time match but the name or end time differs slightly. Compare your local record with the national listing. Update the name or time if needed.
AAGBDB ? Possible match — day and time match but the name is too different to be confident. Check whether this is the same meeting as the national entry. May need investigation.
Not Listed Local only — not found in the national AAGBDB listing. Expected for online-only meetings. For in-person meetings, this may mean registration with AA GB is needed. Contact the GSO.
💡
The reconciliation runs automatically each time the dashboard loads. After updating a meeting's name or time, refresh the dashboard to see the updated badge.

Shortcodes

Amber registers four shortcodes that can be placed in any WordPress page or post. They display live position data — when a position is updated in the admin, the page updates automatically. No manual editing is required.

ShortcodeWhat it displays
[directory_list]A full table of all positions with current holders, email links, and rotation status. Use this on the main Positions or Service Roles page.
[position_header]The position title, minimum sobriety requirement, term length, and email link for a single position.
[position_state]A rotation status badge for a single position — for example, a Vacant badge when the role is empty.
[position_summary]The rich-text summary of a position's duties.
✓
No manual website updates needed. Because shortcodes pull directly from the database, updating a member or position record in the admin is all you need to do. The website reflects the change immediately.

To find where shortcodes are placed on the site, go to Pages in the WordPress sidebar and search for pages containing [position or [directory.

Troubleshooting

Anonymous name rejected as a duplicate

The system requires every anonymous name to be unique. If you see a red warning as you type, the name is already in use by another member record.

Go to Intergroup › Members, sort by Anonymous Name, and find who already has that name. Discuss with both members to agree on a variation — for example, adding an initial. Update the record for whichever member is taking the new variation.

Position not updating on the public site

Check you clicked Update rather than navigating away. Clear the WordPress cache if your site uses a caching plugin. Force-refresh the browser on the public page (Ctrl+Shift+R or Cmd+Shift+R).

Also confirm the position record has a member assigned and that the member's post status is Published, not Draft.

GSR name not appearing in the meeting picker

Open the member record and confirm that Is GSR is ticked and Home Group is set to the correct group. Click Update.

Return to the intergroup meeting record and refresh the page. The GSR name should now appear in brackets next to the group name in the selector.

A meeting shows as Not Listed

Check the meeting's name, day, and start time against the national AAGB listing. If the meeting is in the national listing but showing as Not Listed, the name, day, or time in your local record may differ. Update the local meeting to match.

If the meeting is genuinely absent from the national listing and is an in-person meeting, it may need to be registered. Contact the GSO or the national web team.

Attendance records are missing for a meeting

Open the intergroup meeting record and click Update — even without making changes. This re-triggers the background sync that creates attendance records.

If a group or officer was added and removed before the first save, re-add them and save again.

Rotation date shows as Not Set

If a position shows No Rotation, the rotation date has not been entered for the current officer's term.

Open the position record, enter the expected end date in the Rotation Date field — typically the election date plus the term length in years — and click Update.

The badge will update to the correct status immediately.

The Intergroup menu is missing from the sidebar

Your user account may not have the required permission level. Contact the site administrator to have your role updated. Alternatively, the Amber plugin may have been deactivated — check Plugins in the WordPress admin to confirm it is active alongside Unity and Scrutiny.
